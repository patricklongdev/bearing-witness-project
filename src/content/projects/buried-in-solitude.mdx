<p>The archive contains projects which have endeavoured to describe for a contemporary Canadian audience, through a variety of mixed-media, the continuing practice of solitary confinement. Aleksandra Petkovic, for the 2010 Penal Policy Seminar, painted her vision of Charles Dickens condemnation. As she describes in her Artist Statement she painted in the midst of the Conservative Harper government’s legislative tightening of the screws of imprisonment.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>In the face of falling crime rates, Prime Minister Stephen Harper's government is pushing forward federal legislation to imprison more offenders for longer. The Conservative government's policies manifestly rely on society's ignorance about crime in Canada, the justice system, and modern corrections. In a land that prides itself on rights and equality, this painting represents the elusive distance between the inside and the outside. The prison is the component of the criminal justice system that is concealed from our sight, veiled by a range of oversight practices. Least exposed, it is also the least susceptible to public accountability, so that human rights can continue to be trampled on behind the prison walls. The black surrounding the cell serves to symbolize this shortage of rules and want for external scrutiny. Moreover, the painting epitomizes the modern practice of segregation, but also more generally embodies the frighteningly repressive nature of imprisonment, and the fate of those who just get lost in the system. The bleak darkness encircling the nameless, faceless inmate signifies the depraved prisoner, left alone, caged like an animal, with all four walls a dead end. The bars have no ceiling, denoting limitless restrain and immeasurable confinement.The deafening silence contains him. He shows no signs of physical torture or harm to the body; the skin is free and clear of any wounds conspicuous to the eye. But his scars cut deep. Buried in solitude, his profound agony and acute suffering are irrefutable. The sensory deprivation and social dissection have psychologically crippled and defeated him. Lying in a foetal position at the crux of his cell, the suffering inflicted upon him and which so overpowers him is inconceivable by none but he himself. Those who formulate the system cannot comprehend or even begin to appreciate what they are effecting by designing the most detrimental and devastating experience that subsists within the country.<sup data-fn="99970e27-76f1-4d50-940d-452b109f0b8b" class="fn"><a href="#99970e27-76f1-4d50-940d-452b109f0b8b" id="99970e27-76f1-4d50-940d-452b109f0b8b-link">1</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
