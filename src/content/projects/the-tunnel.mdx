Alisa Bell, in her poem “The Tunnel” composed for the 2012 Prisoners' Rights seminar enlarges the concept of imprisonment to encompass the experience of both victims and prisoners.

> This is a poem about the "prison" experienced by victims and prisoners ( often one and the same) who engage in a sort of macabre dance of being "imprisoned" by the other, whether in perception, identity, and/ or reality.

Here are the first verses.

> ### The Tunnel
> Here, in the tunnel,\
> Cold, sightless, sleeping,\
> As in the graves,\
> As life becomes,\
> Became,\
> In time,\
> Forever and fleeting,\
> This way
>
> Woken by echoes above,\
> Where life continues,\
> Curious - alien - sounds;\
> Secret squeals,\
> Muffled speech,\
> Pitch beyond range,\
> Words indiscernible,\
> Dialogue, forming,\
> Through connections belonging,\
> To a different world,\
> Person, time, place,\
> Above, beyond and beyond,\
> This dark tunnel space
>
> Here, in the tunnel,\
> There is only\
> Darkness, coldness, echoes of living, Wailing above,\
> Where incessant shuffling\
> Of feet prevail;\
> Hurried feet,\
> Slowed feet,\
> Crippled feet;\
> This way,\
> That way,\
> Like sharks,\
> They never stop moving\
> Here in the tunnel,\
> Bewildered,\
> Searching,\
> Trying to comprehend\
> Sordid life,\
> Searching
>
> "For what?" I ask, suddenly forgetting.\
> "Why, you're searching," reminds a Tunnel neighbour,\
> "For the Ghost\
> Who stole your soul,"\
> She says matter of fact.\
> Indeed,\
> How could I forget?\
> How did she know?
>
> I heard he is faceless,\
> And lives in these sewers,\
> I heard he is draped in a dark, protracted cloak, Woven by a balding midget,\
> With blank, blinking eyes,\
> Bright purple lips,\
> And a copper soul\
> That bleeds carroty.\
> Here, in the tunnel,\
> Trapped in moments\
> I wish to outlive,\
> Here, in moments,\
> Relived.
>
> "But feel here!" I say,\
> "I don't want to be here!" I beg to be heard\
> "I want my soul back!\
> Do you hear?"\
> Do they hear?
>
> I listen,\
> Waiting on every sound, Weighting every sound,\
> Until I recognize the pattern And realize,\
> "I don't need him\
> To see him!\
> The faceless Ghost,\
> Lives - I hear him - Everywhere\
> And nowhere."
