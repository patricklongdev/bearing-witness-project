<p>The issue that has animated many conflicts between Indigenous peoples and federal and provincial and governments and corporate Canada is the large scale resource development on lands that Indigenous people assert are subject to their Aboriginal title or protected by treaty. These conflicts have been the subject of protests, acts of resistance and litigation. The archive includes a number of projects that document and illuminate the issues underlying these conflicts and the way they have been portrayed in the media and addressed by the Canadian courts and governments. The 1990 “Oka Crisis” as it has become known, riveted public and international attention. Its genesis was a proposed expansion of a nine hole golf course on land the Mohawks of Kanesatake reserve claimed as a site of a burial ground. In the face of government refusal to cancel the proposed development the Mohawks set up a series of blockades interrupting the traffic into Montréal and Quebec’s provincial police, Sûreté du Québec (SQ), was called in. The crisis escalated when an SQ officer died during a raid in 1990 after Mohawks south of Montreal blocked the Mercier Bridge. The RCMP and Canadian Armed Forces were asked to assist Quebec police. Anishnaaabe artist and scholar <strong>Don Ahnahnsisi McIntyre</strong>, whose multi-media piece “<a href="/projects/ndn-problem/" data-type="link" data-id="/projects/ndn-problem/">NDN Problem</a>” is appears in the Treaty Rights section, has painted his interpretation of the OKA encounter.</p>

<p>This painting which is in my private collection was not done as part of my seminars although Don’s NDA project was. However, Don has consented to my using the painting in the website as it was part of the inspiration for <a href="/projects/thirty-years-forward/" data-type="link" data-id="/projects/thirty-years-forward/">Isabelle Lafroy’s project</a> twenty years later.</p>

<p>In his Artist’s Statement Don writes:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>This piece invites a return to the discourse surrounding the Kanesatake Resistance AKA the Oka Crisis. A land dispute between Mohawks and the town of Oka, Quebec regarding sacred and burial lands that had been sold to a developer for luxury condos and an expansion of the 9-hole golf course. The dispute began on July 11, 1990, and ended September 26, 1990, lasting 77 days.</p>

<p>The media played a huge part in creating the narrative surrounding the events on the ground. One of the most famous images is a photograph by Shaney Komulainenf in which Canadian Pte. Patrick Cloutier and Anishinaabe Brad Larocque stand in opposition to one another, staring each other down. The iconic image is, here, transformed to articulate two warriors coming together as warriors. It moves the narrative from us versus them to warriors illuminated by their shared understanding of being on the battlefield together. Once called upon to act as warrior, their outer personas blend together, and they are connected by the territory on which they make their stand.</p>
</blockquote>
