<p>Yun Li for her project in the 2010 Penal Policy seminar also endeavoured to counterbalance the dark side of the force by developing a Calendar of Hope. Using both her own and friend Scott Clayton’s photographs of landscapes as the backdrop, she selected inspirational messages for prisoners. Her original plan was to have one photograph and quote for all 365 days of the year but ultimately this was scaled back to 100 days largely due to the size limitations of using a Rolodex format. Yun’s unique calendar “100 Days of Hope” assemblies photographs with vistas that take prisoners outside the walls and fences and bring messages from a cast of characters that include Aristotle, Ludwig van Beethoven, the Dalai Lama, Albert Einstein, Nelson Mandela, Franklin D. Roosevelt and Arne Scharzenegger. Other quotes are from decisions of the Supreme Court of Canada, commissions of inquiry, human rights documents and some of my own writing in <em>Justice behind the Walls</em>. We have only photographed a selection of the photographs” and messages in the calendar some of which are reproduced below and the rest can be viewed in the project PDF.</p>
