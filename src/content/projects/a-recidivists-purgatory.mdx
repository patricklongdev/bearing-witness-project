<p>Todd Shikaze, for the 2011 Penal Policy seminar, building upon his class visit to Matsqui, built a three dimensional model of a prison cell. Titled “The Living Graves of Crime: A Recidivist's Purgatory”. Todd’s Artist’s statement explains the ideas and the hours that went into this unique creative work:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>In 1977, the House of Commons Sub-Committee on the Penitentiary System in Canada described Canadian prisons as "living graves of crime" for their failure to either reform prisoners or to protect society. In 2013, we travelled to Matsqui Institution, a federal medium-security prison, to experience first-hand what the Sub-Committee meant. While much has changed since 1977, the loathing in the voices of the inmates when speaking about their relationships with parole officers in particular inspired me to build a model "living grave of crime". The picture the inmates painted was of an abrasive system that quite simply failed to help once an inmate was released; they expressed feelings of contempt towards parole officers who they described as routing against their success. I am hoping to capture the despair of some of the voices in the model I built. The model, titled "The Living Graves of Crime: A Recidivist's Purgatory", is a cross-section of a grave plot. While the surface of the model is a grassy cemetery, the front of the cross­ section reveals that below a tombstone that reads "Here Lies the Living Grave of Crime", there is a jail cell populated by a single unidentifiable man sitting with his head down in his arms. There is a light behind the tombstone that lights up the jail cell from above, casting a shadow of cell bars over its inhabitant. Please be careful with the model as many hours were spent building the furniture and the cell, and there are many small parts that may dislodge easily. Do not leave the model directly in the hot sunlight, as the glue holding everything in place will melt if over-heated and items will fall off the walls. If anything does fall off, the lid is easily removed by lifting.</p>
</blockquote>
