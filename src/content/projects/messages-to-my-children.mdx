<p>Several projects in the Archive, are presentations in both my Aboriginal and Treaty Rights and Penal Policy Seminars not just to me as a law professor but are addressed to future generations. Tyra Soosay, at the time she participated in the 2020 Aboriginal and Treaty Rights Litigation Seminar was expecting her first child. Her essay and the vibrant painting that accompanied it were addressed to that child:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>I think it is more important that Indigenous babies know who they are from their ancestor’s eyes and where they fit into the world (not from a colonizers view). Here is what I would rather my children know about who they are;</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The paintings background is red to represent Indigenous peoples color in the medicine wheel. We are the red nation, and Creator gave us a gift before we came to the Earth, and that gift is that we are spiritual people.</li>

<li>The black border around the painting represents the galaxies that surround us and is filled with stars because we are Star People. Within the galaxy there are star people and spiritual beings. These beings are within the Sundance lodge (located at the bottom of the rainbow). These spirits are spiritual beings who give us true connection, so that we can access our ancestral knowledge and be reminded of who we are and what we are meant to become in this world. We are all spiritual beings having a spiritual experience, and the more connected to Creator we are the better protected we become by her helpers in these lodges.</li>

<li>There is a star directly north of the Sundance lodge that is gold, and this star represents Star Woman. She is connected and sitting closely to Grandmother Spider. Grandmother Spider has both a connection to the galaxies and a connection to the womb. She is sitting in between both worlds. Inside the womb that Grandmother Spider is connected to, is a flame of fire that represents the spirits of our beautiful children who are sitting with Creator. Creator sits with children before they exist their mother’s womb, and this is why children are the most spiritual human beings on Earth. They are the closest to the Creator.</li>

<li>The womb is surrounded by mama’s spirit colors (the colors of the rainbow) and represent the protection and spiritual action of becoming one with your baby. Pregnancy is a ceremony. The rainbow is a symbol of spiritual life essence and spiritual action of creating life.</li>

<li>Around the painting is the sun, moon, and elements of earth. There is an Eagle feather with a baby Eagle feather plume, representing babies Anishinaabe grandmother (Changing Feather Rock). There is corn and a Blackfoot design (symbol). Overtime more Blackfoot, Cree, Anishinaabe, and Dene designs will be added to convey the beauty of our baby’s ancestral beauty.</li>
</ul>

<p>You are unconditionally loved. I am your mother, in Anishinaabe my spirit name sounds like “Aki ka pay mooh sah dang” The One Who Walks the Earth, and you will hear people call me Tyra Soosay. My colours are of the rainbow. Your father’s spirit name is Cree and sounds like “Obi wan knock mow” Whirlwind Blizzard (later Blizzard King), and you will hear people call him Jonas Soosay. We are of four Indigenous nations in addition to being husband and wife, and we are here when you need help forever and always. Your mother is Anishinaabe from your Grandmother (who is from Sagamock/Whitesands in Ontario where your family from your Grandmother/Great Grandmother are Bear Clan, Mermaid Clan, and your Great Grandfather is Thunderbird and Eagle Clan) and from your grandfather you are Sahtu Dene (from Deline, Northwest Territories, Caribou Clan). Our Dene ancestry goes back a known span of four generations: Doug Dillion, Elsie Madeleine (Jimmy Dillion), Alexis (Christine Dolphus), and Henri Takazo (Lucie Tsoukon). Our Dene bloodline is strong, your spiritual strength comes from your ancestry. Don’t forget to feast your ancestors and spirit helpers. Your father is Blackfoot from your Grandmother (she is from the Pikani Nation between Brocket-Alberta and Montana USA), and Cree from your Grandfather (whose territories exist on what is now known as Onion Lake First Nation, on the Saskatchewan-Alberta border, in addition to Metis ancestry). Your Blackfoot Great-Grandmother, Mrs. Man Who Smokes (Peigan) wore the headdress Sundance (Natoas) bundle. Your family are Sundance people, your family are pipe carriers, your family are sweat lodge carriers, your family will show you the methodologies and way of knowing that makes us whole as Indigenous people. We are your teachers, but we don’t own you, because you are your own spirit. The only thing you own in this world is your body, it is the physical shell you were given by the Creator. In Sundance you can pierce and give flesh offerings, because this is the only things on Earth that you can sacrifice to be bonded to the spirit world. We have existed on this Earth called Turtle Island since time immemorial. Like your ancestors you will form a bond to Creator, and hold sacred knowledge of your own that is unique to you and the Great Spirit.</p>

<p>We will be the ones to go back to the old ones and ask questions, your grandparents are ceremonial people and knowledge keepers so they will ground all that we will come to know and pass on together. We will be the ones who go back to the old ways as a family unit. We will keep gathering ancestral Indigenous knowledge together. As children of Anishinaabe people you are corn and wild rice growers and collectors. Your people have special relations to many different lands, foods, and beings across Turtle Island. I hope that we are always surrounded by the beauty of Dene, Blackfoot, Anishinaabe, and Cree culture and designs.</p>

<p>Our home fire will be built on the beauty of being Indigenous, so that we are always radically intimate to the beauty within us and our nations since the beginning of time. We will teach you the laws of this land that have guided our communities and nations. We are urban Indigenous people, so we will learn about the common law (civil law), international law, and Indigenous law living together on equal footing. Our focus as a family must be Indigenous law: the sacred law, the essential laws for our communities today, and customary law to ensure that our relationships with our kin across Turtle Island are strong.</p>

<p>If you want to learn more about your Clans then you have to feast, dream, go to ceremonies, and invest time into your family’s knowledge base. Through the spirit world you can gain back your Clan knowledge, and bring it back to our family and communities, so that we remember what it means to have Star Being knowledge.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>In an appendix to her essay Tyra provided a cautionary note of colonial history to which her painting and her essay were a counterpoint:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>In every space of Turtle Island Indigenous nations had Clans. Some other places in the world forgot their Clan knowledge and took a different path. Colonization tried to take over our Clan knowledge. There are parts of history (especially in Canada) that do not define who you are, and this industrial complex knowledge system of how we come to know ourselves is secondary to your ancestral knowledge as an Indigenous person walking in this world. You are worthy. You are not white, and you never have to measure up to a white idea. Your path was set with Creator and Grandmother Spider and many other Spirits, before you entered this galaxy on which you started your path. This “logic driven” institutional knowledge can be used as a tool kit to fight colonization theory and worldview, but it is not something that I want you to internalize or keep as a part of your primary ways of knowing and being in this world. This knowledge is often rife with bias, stereotypes, and ultimately you can lose something within its racist and sexist violence. You are a whole person, with a full heart, so walk with your head held high my babies. I give you permission to walk away and to survive until you thrive.</p>

<p>I caution you that the laws of land, we currently call Canada, continue to be under a colonial regime that enables colonization to remain an all indoctrinating force. It is a legal and political history that we need to change (with our allies) to reflect the truth of our humanity as Indigenous people. Colonization is brainwashing on a social, cultural, and physiological level. Be bigger than control, domination, and fear. We as human beings deserve to be healthy and happy. The Doctrine of Discovery and Nazi race theory are ideas that contaminate human beings and become infections in society. The people (settlers/colonizers) who came to our lands committed spiritual rape and genocide of Indigenous people, over our knowledge, laws, governance, and lives. The Doctrine of Discovery and Terra nullius (land that is not occupied) are theories and ideas that are diseases to effective democracy and society today. The ideas were brought forth through Christianity and the man-made law by self-proclaimed Christians didn’t see Indigenous people as human beings. The Christian mission of popes, kings and queens, god (apparently), and explorers dramatically changed what they called the New World (later called North America). As far back as the 5th century Churches began to establish Christian commonwealth through international law and papal jurisdiction that under Discovery principles non-Christians did not possess the same human/sacred rights to land, sovereignty, and self determination as Christian people. Non-Christians (infidels) had to comply with European conceptions of law. By 1240 the Church could deprive pagans of their property or sovereignty if they failed to admit Christian missionaries or defied European natural law across the world. By 1455 slavery of Pagans and property theft was allowed against non- Christian owners. These laws were transported over to Turtle Island. The British, French, and other colonies used the Doctrine of Discovery to take part in the largest land theft known to this land. There are many other things that were stolen in addition to the land. This land mass is governed currently by common law. Common law is English law and even Aboriginal law is established from European ways of thinking and knowing. We have allies, sometimes, in these systems, but these systems are still rife with colonial ideologies, so you need to remember this is not OUR law. We have our own legal systems, and your parents will show you so you can show your babies. These are man made laws, by judges who are just like us, pitiful humans. Aboriginal law has existed for less than a hundred years now, but our laws on Turtle Island have been inside us since the beginning of time.</p>

<p>Remember that we may have intergenerational trauma BUT we also have intergenerational resilience in our blood memory. The worse crimes committed against humanity won’t break us. Residential schools didn’t break us. The 60’s scoop won’t break us. The Doctrine of Discovery cannot break us. Why? Because we want to live. We want to be here, where Creator placed us with the Star Beings, and we want to live long healthy and beautiful lives like we are meant to. Your ancestors love you unconditionally. We love you unconditionally, forever and always, until the end of time, and into the galaxies and spaces in time we have yet to know.</p>
</blockquote>
