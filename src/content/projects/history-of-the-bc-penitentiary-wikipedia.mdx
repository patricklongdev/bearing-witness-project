<p>Nathan Ryan, in the 2014 Penal Policy Seminar submitted the first <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Columbia_Penitentiary">Wikipedia</a> entry on the history of the BC penitentiary. Nathan in his reflections essay describes the challenges and opportunities represented by this project.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>Writing this article proved to be a much longer and more difficult process than I anticipated. I have always been good at writing academic papers - they always come out well and I can write them fairly quickly - so I assumed that writing an encyclopedia entry would be a breeze. After all, they are short and written in accessible language, so I thought that it would be a straightforward process. I was completely wrong.<br />It turns out that writing something so short poses its own difficulties. This is because, despite being shorter than an academic paper, the subject that it must cover - an institution that existed for over a century and that continues to cast a shadow over the Canadian carceral landscape - is extremely broad. Many things must be included. The great difficulty, for me, was in making exclusions. This article could have continued to grow beyond book length without making harsh editing choices. Topics had to be sufficiently analyzed to give meaning, but yet I had to constantly fight my desires to go into great detail or too in-depth an analysis, or the final product would not have been acceptable. There were a number of incidents at the BC Penitentiary that could easily merit an essay or more all on their own. For example, my final submissions on the Doukhobors came in at less than a page; however, this was the result of distilling down two days and hundreds of pages of research, countless pages of notes, and my having written enough material to comprise an essay of moderate length. It was very difficult for me to completely cut out paragraphs and pages that I had written, but this was necessary to produce an article that is appropriate for an encyclopaedia.</p>

<p>The challenges I encountered were exacerbated by the fact that there is comparatively little original research available about the BC Penitentiary. As an encyclopaedia, Wikipcdia is designed to convey a summary of existing knowledge on its topics. It is not designed as a platform to publish original research. Moreover, it prefers secondary sources to primary ones. This made finding appropriate sources quite difficult. I was, in fact, not able to escape primary sources; indeed I am incredible thankful for some of them, notably a Malcom Mathcson's UBC Masters Thesis from the 1950s that conducted a detailed survey of BC Penitentiary. The best source for my purposes was a book by Jack Scott called Four Walls in the West. It contained a wealth of information about various aspects of the penitentiary. It was well-written, but was organized in a terribly haphazard manner from the point of view of someone conducting research. (It makes a great casual read, however; my girlfriend, who is not a law student nor ordinarily interested in penal policy whatsoever, has asked me to delay returning it to the library so that she can read it!) Your book, <em>Prisoners of Isolation</em>, was incredibly helpful as a source of criticism of the Penitentiary.</p>

<p>Choosing to write an encyclopaedia article imposed certain limitations on what topics I could write about. For example, I was considering compiling lists of escapes and of notable inmates. However, there were no adequate secondary sources for this information, so it would have required a survey of primary sources; this would have constituted original research and would not have been appropriate for publication in an encyclopaedia such as Wikipedia. My natural tendency would have been to write about the experiences of the prisoners rather than about the institution itself. While limited use of such analysis was essential to give meaning to some parts of my essay (such as the part concerning solitary confinement), an in-depth analysis of prisoners' experiences was not appropriate for an encyclopaedia entry. This resulted in a sharp learning curve and a great deal of research unutilized in the published article. For instance, I wrote a draft section comparing the daily experience of inmates at BC Penitentiary throughout the decades, and how those experiences would have changed and remained the same.</p>

<p>Unfortunately, I had to delete that entire part, because that was an analysis of penal policy too far removed from the proper scope of an article about a former prison.</p>

<p>The most surprising thing about this experience was how much time it has taken (and is continuing to take) to deal with technology issues relating to Wikipedia. Wikipedia is a sleek-looking website. It has well over three million English articles, and millions of layman editors and contributors. l thus expected the article uploading process to essentially consist of copying and pasting, with some minor formatting adjustments. l turned out to be wildly wrong about this. The formatting and uploading systems that Wikipedia uses arc extremely unfriendly to the user. I am sure that I will get much faster at formatting articles in the future, but, this being my first, it is taking me several hours to get everything correctly formatted and reviewed on the site before it can go live.</p>

<p>For all the unexpected difficulty I faced writing this article, I am unreservedly glad that I undertook it. Wikipedia has proven itself to be one of the most important learning tools ever created. Through it, people have access to a wealth of information on millions of topics. It is expanding each day, and any knowledgeable person can contribute a new article or improve an existing one. Best of all, it is free and accessible. One of the most useful things that academics can contribute to society is to use their intellectual abilities to educate the public. Wikipedia is an invaluable arm of this public academic function.</p>

<p>The state of the Wikipedia articles relating to Canadian prisons is quite poor. My hope is that this article serves as a template for future articles about the Canadian prison system and penal policy, and that it encourages contributions from other knowledgeable people.</p>
</blockquote>
