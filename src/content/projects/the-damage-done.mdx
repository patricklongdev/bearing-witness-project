<p>The bold text of Sarah’s zine is given personal reference with the inclusion in her overview of the poem ‘Dear Cocaine’ written by an Aboriginal prisoner:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>Addiction has a particular power to cause those caught in its grips to behave in ways they normally wouldn't. It is characterized in part by an obsession with using a substance to the extent that it has unwanted affects on one's life.. At the same time, an addict's chosen substance can provide a way to deal with difficult internal and external circumstances. For Lorri­ Anne Cope, an Aboriginal woman who did a six-year sentence at the Okimaw Ohci Healing Lodge in Saskatchewan, described the conflicted relationship an addict has with their substance in a poem titled "Dear Cocaine":</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>… At this point in my life, I don't miss you but at one point in my life I felt you were my only support. I'd like to say thank-you for helping me through my hard times. You helped me numb most of my hurt. You helped me forget most of my troubles but this was only temporary relief. We had quite the relationship and have done some crazy things together. You gave me power then that I would never need again.</p>

<p>Now I see you as a weakness. Throughout your support I forgot my inner strengths and I also forgot how important I am to my loved ones. I became dependent on you In the company of you I let almost all the things that were important to me slip away and none of that mattered to me as long as I had you. Then I realized where I was and why I was there. I began to think about reality and how wrong our relationship was. I began to think about what I let slip out of my reach and to be honest it was just about everything in my life.</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
