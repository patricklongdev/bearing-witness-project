Lindsay Ann Lee’s poem “Nice to meet you” written for the 2005 Prisoners' Rights seminar is the shortest in the Archive but based upon Lyndsay’s meeting with a prisoner at Matsqui enigmatically expresses the connection with, yet distance between, student and prisoner.

> ### Nice to meet you
> your shirt is from nineteen seventy-one\
> i dropped a mitten and you picked it up with the comer\
> of your shirt, not letting our skin\
> touch, as though leprosy were alive and well\
> on your island
>
> i didn't know i'd missed you until yesterday, out there in a cage that\
> suspends my own\
> we have needs, sky train rides, chocolate, vitamins,\
> clean sheets, gentle hands on our body at night\
> moving
>
> you have had flags rammed into your heart and i stand colonized\
> by choice\
> that time someone knifed you in your cave & you killed them, that time i ran\
> through the park alone at 3am & no one took me down\
> (the drama of human life always sounds so ludicrous on paper)
>
> your soliloquy is as foreign and familiar as Shakespeare-of Romeo, nephews Brutus and intercepted mail, fucking and rotten knee-breaking shame
>
> i wanted to bring you my kittens
>
> when arbitrary is your daddy and you want to write a song but\
> no one noticed your piano fingers in time\
> you said you know the Chinese mafia, dream of the days when you shovelled\
> horse manure in spring, want to learn about welding and psychology, once sold crack from a beautiful wooden box\
> you said don't ever put the keys in the ignition and get into the car with the doors unlocked, don't ever
>
> we are not of a different species, but that's how you looked at me when i walked away
>
> For the prisoners of Matsqui, November 2005
