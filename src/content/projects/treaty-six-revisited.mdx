<p>Alexis Omichinski in her 2010 project “Treaty Six Revisited” for the Aboriginal and Treaty Rights Seminar reviewed the context of the treaty, the importance of Indigenous protocol and examined the Elders’ interpretation of the treaty provisions which maintain that the session of land was only the surface rights required for farming and agricultural purposes and was not a general surrender of the rights of the Treaty Six Nations.</p>

<p>Alexis, after reviewing the literature about Treaty Six, including research based on elders’ interviews, proposes a re-writing of the treaty text which better reflects the terms agreed by the Treaty Nations.</p>

<p>Alexis’ proposed revisions to the treaty reflect the Indigenous understanding and relate to the scope of what was ceded to the Crown which, according to the Elders interviews, where only surface rights as required for farming and agricultural purposes:</p>

<p>As with other projects Alexis give emphasis to the Indigenous treaty protocols, particularly the Pipe Ceremony.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-gallery has-nested-images columns-default is-cropped">
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/assets/2025/02/Alexis_Omichinski_07.jpg" alt="Alexis Omichinski's 2010 project titled “Treaty Six Revisited” detail " class="wp-image-3905"/></figure>
</figure>

<p>The treaty text contains no reference to the pipe ceremony. Alexis proposes that the text</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>Whereas the said Commissioners have proceeded to negotiate a treaty with the said Indians in the same has been finally agreed upon and concluded as follows, that is to say:</p>
</blockquote>

<p>should read:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>And whereas, having shared the secret pipe, the treaty negotiations between said Commissioners and said Indians have commenced, and the said Commissioners and the said Indians have proceeded on the undertaking that all promises spoken from the moment the pipe was shared become covenants between the First Nations, the Crown, and the deity.<sup data-fn="f79ecba0-f571-4fe3-a933-739ca5731daf" class="fn"><a href="#f79ecba0-f571-4fe3-a933-739ca5731daf" id="f79ecba0-f571-4fe3-a933-739ca5731daf-link">1</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
