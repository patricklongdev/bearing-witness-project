<p>Lindsay Ann Lee’s poem “Nice to meet you” written for the 2005 Penal Policy seminar is the shortest in the archive but based upon Lyndsay’s meeting with a prisoner at Matsqui enigmatically expresses the connection with, yet distance between, student and prisoner.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Nice to meet you</h3>

<p>your shirt is from nineteen seventy-one<br />i dropped a mitten and you picked it up with the comer<br />of your shirt, not letting our skin<br />touch, as though leprosy were alive and well<br />on your island</p>

<p>i didn't know i'd missed you until yesterday, out there in a cage that<br />suspends my own<br />we have needs, sky train rides, chocolate, vitamins,<br />clean sheets, gentle hands on our body at night<br />moving</p>

<p>you have had flags rammed into your heart and i stand colonized<br />by choice<br />that time someone knifed you in your cave &amp; you killed them, that time i ran<br />through the park alone at 3am &amp; no one took me down<br />(the drama of human life always sounds so ludicrous on paper)</p>

<p>your soliloquy is as foreign and familiar as Shakespeare-of Romeo, nephews Brutus and intercepted mail, fucking and rotten knee-breaking shame</p>

<p>i wanted to bring you my kittens</p>

<p>when arbitrary is your daddy and you want to write a song but<br />no one noticed your piano fingers in time<br />you said you know the Chinese mafia, dream of the days when you shovelled<br />horse manure in spring, want to learn about welding and psychology, once sold crack from a beautiful wooden box<br />you said don't ever put the keys in the ignition and get into the car with the doors unlocked, don't ever</p>

<p>we are not of a different species, but that's how you looked at me when i walked away</p>

<p>For the prisoners of Matsqui, November 2005</p>
</blockquote>
