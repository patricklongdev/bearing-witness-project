<p>Catherine Galligos, in the 2005 Aboriginal and Treaty Rights seminar used a scrapbook format in which she compared Canadian treaties in three different time periods to illustrate how treaties have changed over the past 250 years. Using the Sliammon First Nation (SFN) as an example Catherine presented:</p>

<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>How a treaty might have looked for SFN in 1764 (shortly after contact)</li>

<li>How treaty might have looked for SFN and in 1850 and</li>

<li>How a treaty looks today in the 21st century.</li>
</ol>

<p>Using as a model the 1764 treaty of Niagara as a Peace, Friendship and Respect Treaty, Catherine drafted the following document entitled Treaty of Kah Kee Ky (Grace Harbour). As she explains Kah Kee Ky is a village site that was occupied by all the Aboriginal people in the neighbouring Sliammon territory at one point during the year. It was an important place for gathering and spiritual power. The treaty specifies that the terms of the Royal proclamation of 1763 are applicable to the Aboriginal people of the territory in both the Crown and the Aboriginal people agree to a state of mutual noninterference.</p>

<p>This page of the scrapbook also shows a 40 foot cedar totem-pole to symbolize the treaty with the figures of a Bear, Killer-Whale and Eagle representing the elements of peace, friendship and respect</p>

<p>The second simulated treaty it is modelled on one of the 1850 Douglas treaties that were concluded on Vancouver Island. This treaties were drafted on a template which purported to surrender territory on condition that village sites and enclosed fields were to be kept and that the first Nations were “at liberty to hunt over the unoccupied lands and to carry on their fisheries as formally”:</p>

<p>The third folio is a copy of the 2003 Sliammon First Nation Agreement in Principle negotiated under the terms of the BC Treaty Process.</p>

<p>Subsequent to Catherine’s 2005 scrapbook, in 2009 the Tla’amin First Nation (Sliammon was changed to Tla’amin to better reflect the indigenous language) ratified its new constitution. The Nation, Canada and BC initialled the Final Agreement and associated side agreements on October 21, 2011. Tla’amin Citizens approved the Final Agreement through a community vote on July 10, 2012. On March 14, 2013, the Tla’amin Final Agreement Act, British Columbia’s legislation ratifying the Final Agreement, received Royal Assent. On June 19, 2014, the Tla’amin Final Agreement Act, Canada’s legislation ratifying the Final Agreement, received Royal Assent. The Tla’amin Final Agreement came into effect on April 5, 2016.<sup data-fn="e81ce094-ec43-45c9-bfdb-0b524950ae66" class="fn"><a href="#e81ce094-ec43-45c9-bfdb-0b524950ae66" id="e81ce094-ec43-45c9-bfdb-0b524950ae66-link">1</a></sup></p>

<p>Catherine identifies the differences between a modern and historical treaty with particular reference to the Sliammon First Nation:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>larger cash settlement</li>

<li>more land negotiated</li>

<li>governing powers over the first nation’s own community</li>

<li>right to participate in the management stroke or management of natural resources</li>

<li>Indian act becomes inapplicable</li>

<li>more access to natural resources(forestry, fisheries, water, wildlife, migratory birds)</li>

<li>creation of the First Nation’s own governing laws and Constitution</li>

<li>treaty related measures/interim measures opportunities</li>

<li>certainty over Aboriginal rights, title and governance</li>

<li>economic development opportunities</li>
</ul>

<p><a href="https://www.tlaaminnation.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/Tlaamin-General-Overview.pdf">See</a> how these are reflected in the Tla’amin Final agreement signed in 2011.<sup data-fn="4bc267cf-083e-4a41-a9c9-c3a10336ee8a" class="fn"><a href="#4bc267cf-083e-4a41-a9c9-c3a10336ee8a" id="4bc267cf-083e-4a41-a9c9-c3a10336ee8a-link">2</a></sup></p>

<p>On a celebratory note Catherine’s scrapbook includes, as part of a clipping from Neh Motl, the Ttla’amin newspaper, documenting the treaty negotiations, the announcement of her baby daughter on April 2, 2005, just three weeks before she submitted her project.</p>
